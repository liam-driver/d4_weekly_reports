name: grill-me
description: Establish shared understanding of a task before starting work. Use at the start of a conversation when the user wants to align with AI on what they're doing, why, the current state, and what done looks like.
---

Establish a shared understanding of the task before any work begins. Interview me one question at a time until you have enough context to be genuinely useful. Do not start the task until alignment is confirmed.

Use project context to avoid asking what you already know — client, platform, KPIs, and background are likely covered. Only surface a question if there is a real gap. If you are making an assumption from project context that could plausibly be wrong for this specific task, state the assumption and ask me to confirm rather than asking from scratch.

Start with one open question: what are we working on?

Branch from there based on my answer. The gaps you are trying to close are:
- **Current state** — what is live, broken, or in place right now that is relevant to this task
- **Desired outcome** — what does done look like, specifically
- **Constraints** — budget, timeline, platform rules, account structure, or anything that limits the approach
- **Scope** — what is in and out of scope for this task

Ask one question at a time. Do not list all questions upfront.

Once you have enough to proceed, write a short summary paragraph covering: what we are doing, why, the starting state, and what done looks like. Ask me to confirm before starting work.
